REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL
10 MILLION KCAL/HR THERMIC FLUID HEATER SYSTEM
WITH WASTE HEAT RECOVERY

CLIENT: M/s Bharat Textile Mills Ltd.
LOCATION: Surat Textile Park, Pandesara GIDC, Surat, Gujarat - 394221
RFP NO: BTM/PROJ/TFH-10M/2026/R-019
DATE: 10 March 2026
CLOSING: 25 April 2026

SECTION 1: SCOPE
1.1 Supply and commissioning of one (1) thermic fluid heater rated 10 million kcal/hr on coal/biomass firing.
1.2 Complete waste heat recovery system including economizer and air preheater.
1.3 Thermic fluid piping from heater to process battery limits (approximately 500 meters).
1.4 Fuel handling system from yard to heater front.
1.5 Chimney, ducting, and ESP for emission control.
1.6 PLC-based control panel with SCADA.
1.7 Excluded: Thermic fluid procurement (owner supply), civil works, HT/LT electrical panels.

SECTION 2: PROCESS REQUIREMENTS
2.1 Heating Medium: Thermax Thermia Oil (or equivalent synthetic thermic fluid).
2.2 Operating Temperature Range: 280-300 deg C at heater outlet.
2.3 Return Temperature: 240-250 deg C.
2.4 Maximum Film Temperature: <340 deg C to prevent fluid degradation.
2.5 Flow Rate: Approximately 450 m3/hr at operating conditions.
2.6 Coil Pressure Drop: Maximum 3.5 kg/cm2 at design flow.
2.7 Process Application: Textile dyeing, finishing, and polyester processing.
2.8 Continuous Operation: 330 days/year, 24 hours/day.
2.9 Load Variation: 40-100% with smooth modulation.

SECTION 4: TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Heater Design: Multi-pass coil construction with single continuous coil per pass. Minimum 3 passes.
4.2 Combustion System: Traveling grate stoker for coal, provision for biomass co-firing.
4.3 Efficiency: Minimum 82% on GCV basis with economizer and APH.
4.4 Coil Material: SA210 Grade A1 seamless tubes. Minimum 4mm wall thickness. All welds radiographed 100%.
4.5 Expansion Tank: Minimum 10% of total thermic fluid volume. Nitrogen blanketing provision.
4.6 Circulation Pump: Centrifugal, hot oil service, mechanical seal, minimum 1 working + 1 standby.
4.7 Safety Systems: Low flow interlock, high temperature trip, stack temperature alarm, pressure relief valve, fire detection in duct.

SECTION 6: HEAT RECOVERY AND ENERGY AUDIT

6.2 This facility is a Designated Consumer under PAT Scheme (Cycle VII). The heater system must demonstrate specific energy consumption improvement of 3% over baseline.
6.3 Bidder to provide energy balance and Sankey diagram for the proposed system.
6.4 Real-time energy monitoring with daily/monthly report generation.
