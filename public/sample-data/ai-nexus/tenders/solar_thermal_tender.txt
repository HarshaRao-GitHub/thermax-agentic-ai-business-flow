SECTION 1: PROJECT OVERVIEW AND SCOPE
1.1 Design, supply, install, and commission a 5 MW thermal parabolic trough concentrating solar thermal (CST) system to provide industrial process heat for dairy processing operations.
1.2 The system shall generate hot water at 150-180 deg C for pasteurization, CIP, and sterilization processes.
1.3 Complete scope: Parabolic trough collector field, Heat transfer fluid (HTF) system, HTF-to-water heat exchanger, Thermal energy storage (4 hours), Control system with weather station integration, Civil works for collector field.
1.4 The system shall integrate with existing oil-fired boiler as backup. Seamless switchover required.
1.5 Total collector aperture area: approximately 12,000 m2.
1.6 Land available: 5 acres (approximately 20,000 m2) within dairy complex.

SECTION 3: TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Collector Type: Parabolic trough with single-axis tracking (N-S orientation, E-W tracking).
3.2 Collector Aperture Width: 5.7-6.0 meters.
3.3 Receiver Tube: Evacuated tube with cermet selective coating. Absorptance >95%, emittance <10% at 350 deg C.
3.4 Mirror: Silver-back low-iron glass, reflectivity >93%. Thickness 4mm.
3.5 HTF: Therminol VP-1 or equivalent, operating range 15-400 deg C.
3.6 HTF Operating Temperature: 220-300 deg C.
3.7 Heat Exchanger: Shell and tube type, SS 316L tubes, designed for 100% load + 10% margin.
3.8 Thermal Storage: Molten salt (Solar Salt: 60% NaNO3 + 40% KNO3) 2-tank system for 4 hours at 80% load.
3.9 Tracking System: Closed-loop sun tracking with inclinometer + sun sensor. Accuracy: +/- 0.1 degree.
3.10 Stow Position: Automatic stow at wind speed >55 km/hr.

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE GUARANTEES
4.1 Annual Solar-to-Thermal Efficiency: >45% (based on DNI to useful heat delivered).
4.2 Peak Thermal Output: 5 MW at 850 W/m2 DNI.
4.3 Storage Efficiency: >93% round-trip thermal efficiency.
4.4 Plant Availability: >95% during sunshine hours (excluding cleaning and weather).
4.5 Minimum 25-year design life for collector structure, 20 years for receiver tubes.

SECTION 6: SYSTEM INTEGRATION
6.1 The CST system shall integrate with existing 5 TPH oil-fired boiler through common hot water header.
6.2 Automatic switchover: when solar output is insufficient, boiler starts automatically.
6.3 Solar priority mode: boiler operates only to make up deficit.
6.4 Common SCADA interface displaying both solar and boiler parameters.
6.5 Data logging: 1-minute resolution for all solar field parameters. Cloud-based monitoring dashboard.
6.6 API interface for integration with RCDF ERP system (SAP).
