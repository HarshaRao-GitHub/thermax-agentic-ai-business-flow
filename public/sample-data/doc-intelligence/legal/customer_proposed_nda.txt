================================================================================
          CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT
================================================================================

This Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered
into as of __________________ ("Effective Date")

BETWEEN:

(1) MERIDIAN TECHNOLOGIES PTE. LTD., a company incorporated under the laws
    of Singapore, having its registered office at 1 Raffles Place, #44-02,
    One Raffles Place Tower 1, Singapore 048616
    (hereinafter referred to as "Meridian" or the "Disclosing Party")

AND

(2) THERMAX LIMITED, a company incorporated under the laws of India, having
    its registered office at D-13, MIDC Industrial Area, R.D. Aga Road,
    Chinchwad, Pune - 411019, Maharashtra, India
    (hereinafter referred to as "Thermax" or the "Receiving Party")

    (Meridian and Thermax are hereinafter individually referred to as a
    "Party" and collectively as the "Parties")

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                              RECITALS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEREAS, Meridian intends to engage Thermax for the potential supply of
industrial heating and cooling solutions for Meridian's Southeast Asian
manufacturing operations (the "Purpose");

WHEREAS, Meridian will disclose to Thermax certain proprietary, confidential,
and sensitive business information in connection with the Purpose;

WHEREAS, Meridian requires Thermax to maintain the confidentiality of such
information under the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements
contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt
and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as
follows:

================================================================================
              ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all information, data, materials,
    documents, know-how, or other knowledge of any nature whatsoever,
    disclosed by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party to the Receiving
    Party, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, visually,
    or by any other means, and regardless of whether such information is
    marked or identified as "confidential" or "proprietary." For the
    avoidance of doubt, any information shared verbally during meetings,
    telephone calls, video conferences, or any other oral communication
    shall be deemed Confidential Information without the requirement of
    subsequent written confirmation or identification.

1.2 "Disclosing Party" means Meridian Technologies Pte. Ltd. and its
    affiliates, subsidiaries, successors, and assigns.

1.3 "Receiving Party" means Thermax Limited.

1.4 "Representatives" means, with respect to the Receiving Party, all of
    its directors, officers, employees, agents, advisors (including
    attorneys, accountants, consultants, and financial advisors),
    subcontractors, sub-suppliers, service providers, joint venture partners,
    temporary staff, interns, and any other person or entity engaged by or
    acting on behalf of the Receiving Party in any capacity whatsoever,
    whether directly or indirectly.

1.5 "Competitors" means any person, firm, company, or entity that is
    engaged in, or planning to engage in, the design, manufacture, supply,
    or distribution of industrial process equipment, semiconductor
    fabrication facility systems, or cleanroom environmental solutions in
    the Asia-Pacific region.

1.6 "Purpose" means the evaluation, negotiation, and potential consummation
    of the business relationship described in the Recitals, and all related
    activities as determined by the Disclosing Party.

================================================================================
            ARTICLE 2: SCOPE OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
================================================================================

2.1 Confidential Information includes, without limitation, the following:

    (a) Technical Information: all engineering designs, drawings, blueprints,
        specifications, formulas, algorithms, software (including source
        code, object code, APIs, and documentation), technical data,
        research and development activities, prototypes, samples, inventions,
        patent applications (filed and unfiled), manufacturing processes,
        testing methodologies, quality control procedures, equipment
        configurations, and any technical information of any kind;

    (b) Commercial Information: business plans, marketing strategies, sales
        data, customer lists, customer identities, supplier information,
        pricing structures, distribution channels, market analyses,
        competitive intelligence, procurement strategies, vendor assessments,
        and all commercial data;

    (c) Financial Information: financial statements, projections, budgets,
        cost structures, investment plans, revenue data, profit margins,
        credit terms, payment histories, banking information, and any
        financial data whether published or unpublished;

    (d) Intellectual Property: all patents, patent applications, trademarks,
        trade names, copyrights, design rights, database rights, trade
        secrets, know-how, proprietary methodologies, and any other
        intellectual property rights, whether registered or unregistered;

    (e) Trade Secrets: any information that derives independent economic
        value from not being generally known, including but not limited to
        process configurations, system architectures, optimization
        techniques, and operational methodologies;

    (f) Personnel Information: employee data, organizational structures,
        compensation information, key personnel details, recruitment
        strategies, and succession plans;

    (g) Strategic Information: merger and acquisition plans, partnership
        discussions, expansion strategies, facility plans, capital
        expenditure programs, and long-term strategic objectives;

    (h) The existence and terms of this Agreement and the discussions
        between the Parties.

2.2 Confidential Information shall also include any analyses, compilations,
    studies, summaries, notes, or other materials prepared by the Receiving
    Party that contain, reflect, are generated from, or are derived in any
    way from the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party.

2.3 Any information disclosed in connection with the Purpose shall be
    presumed to be Confidential Information unless the Receiving Party can
    demonstrate otherwise in accordance with Article 4.

================================================================================
            ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY
================================================================================

3.1 Standard of Care. The Receiving Party shall:

    (a) Hold and maintain all Confidential Information in the strictest
        confidence and exercise the highest degree of care to protect
        such information, which shall in all cases exceed the degree of
        care that the Receiving Party uses to protect its own confidential
        information;

    (b) Not disclose, publish, or disseminate any Confidential Information
        to any third party without the prior written consent of the
        Disclosing Party;

    (c) Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and not
        for any other purpose whatsoever, whether directly or indirectly;

    (d) Restrict disclosure of the Confidential Information to only those
        Representatives who have an absolute need to know such information
        for the Purpose and who have been pre-approved in writing by the
        Disclosing Party;

    (e) Ensure that all Representatives who receive Confidential Information
        execute individual confidentiality undertakings in a form approved
        by the Disclosing Party prior to receiving any Confidential
        Information;

    (f) Not reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile, analyze, or otherwise
        attempt to derive the composition, structure, design, underlying
        information, or ideas of any Confidential Information;

    (g) Immediately notify the Disclosing Party in writing (within 24 hours)
        upon becoming aware of any unauthorized use, disclosure, or loss
        of Confidential Information, or any suspected breach of this
        Agreement;

    (h) Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational
        security measures, including encryption, access controls, and
        physical security measures, to protect Confidential Information
        from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure;

    (i) Not make copies of the Confidential Information except as strictly
        necessary for the Purpose and with the prior written consent of
        the Disclosing Party.

3.2 The Receiving Party shall be strictly liable for any breach of this
    Agreement by any of its Representatives, regardless of whether such
    breach was authorized by the Receiving Party.

3.3 The Receiving Party shall provide the Disclosing Party with a written
    list of all individuals who have had access to the Confidential
    Information on a quarterly basis.

================================================================================
                    ARTICLE 4: EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

4.1 The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Agreement shall not
    apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by
    clear and convincing documentary evidence:

    (a) Was in the public domain at the time of disclosure, or subsequently
        entered the public domain through no act, omission, or fault of
        the Receiving Party or its Representatives;

    (b) Was lawfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to
        disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as conclusively evidenced by
        contemporaneous written records predating this Agreement;

    (c) Was received by the Receiving Party from a bona fide third party
        who was not under any obligation of confidentiality to the
        Disclosing Party and who had the legal right to disclose such
        information, as evidenced by written documentation;

    (d) Is approved for release or disclosure by the Disclosing Party in
        writing, signed by a duly authorized officer of the Disclosing
        Party.

    [NOTE: No "independently developed" exclusion is included in this
    Agreement.]

4.2 Legally Compelled Disclosure. If the Receiving Party is required by
    applicable law, regulation, legal process, or governmental order to
    disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:

    (a) Provide the Disclosing Party with immediate written notice of such
        requirement (within 24 hours, to the extent legally permitted);

    (b) Cooperate fully with the Disclosing Party, at the Receiving Party's
        cost, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy;

    (c) Disclose only the minimum portion of the Confidential Information
        that is strictly required to be disclosed;

    (d) Use best efforts to obtain reliable assurance that confidential
        treatment will be accorded to the Confidential Information; and

    (e) Permit the Disclosing Party to participate in any proceedings
        related to such compelled disclosure.

================================================================================
                ARTICLE 5: TERM AND DURATION
================================================================================

5.1 Term. This Agreement shall become effective on the Effective Date and
    shall remain in force for a period of three (3) years from the Effective
    Date. This Agreement may only be terminated by mutual written consent of
    both Parties.

5.2 Survival of Obligations. The obligations of confidentiality and non-use
    set forth in this Agreement shall survive the expiration or termination
    of this Agreement for a period of seven (7) years from the date of such
    expiration or termination.

5.3 Trade Secrets. With respect to any Confidential Information that
    constitutes a trade secret under any applicable law in any jurisdiction,
    the obligations of confidentiality shall continue in perpetuity.

================================================================================
        ARTICLE 6: RETURN AND DESTRUCTION OF INFORMATION
================================================================================

6.1 Upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the
    written request of the Disclosing Party at any time, the Receiving
    Party shall promptly (within ten (10) business days) return to the
    Disclosing Party all originals and copies of Confidential Information
    in any form whatsoever.

6.2 The Receiving Party may only destroy Confidential Information in lieu
    of returning it with the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.
    Any approved destruction must be carried out using methods specified or
    approved by the Disclosing Party, and the Receiving Party shall provide
    a detailed certificate of destruction signed by a senior officer of
    the Receiving Party, listing all materials destroyed, the method of
    destruction, and the date of destruction.

6.3 The Receiving Party shall not retain any copies, summaries, extracts,
    or reproductions of the Confidential Information in any form, including
    electronic backups, archival copies, or cloud storage, without the
    express prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

6.4 The Disclosing Party reserves the right to audit the Receiving Party's
    systems and records to verify complete return or destruction of
    Confidential Information in accordance with Article 10 of this
    Agreement.

================================================================================
                ARTICLE 7: NO LICENSE OR IP TRANSFER
================================================================================

7.1 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting any license,
    right, title, or interest in or to any Confidential Information,
    intellectual property, patent, trademark, copyright, trade secret, or
    any other proprietary right of the Disclosing Party.

7.2 All Confidential Information shall remain the sole and exclusive
    property of the Disclosing Party. The Receiving Party hereby assigns
    and agrees to assign to the Disclosing Party all right, title, and
    interest in any intellectual property, inventions, or works of
    authorship created by the Receiving Party using or derived from the
    Confidential Information.

7.3 The Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty, express or
    implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any purpose
    of the Confidential Information.

================================================================================
                        ARTICLE 8: REMEDIES
================================================================================

8.1 The Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that any breach or
    threatened breach of this Agreement will cause irreparable harm to
    the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages alone would be an
    inadequate remedy.

8.2 The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek immediate injunctive
    relief, specific performance, or other equitable remedies in any court
    of competent jurisdiction without the necessity of proving actual
    damages, posting any bond or other security, or establishing the
    inadequacy of monetary damages.

8.3 The rights and remedies provided herein are cumulative and in addition
    to any other rights or remedies available at law or in equity.

================================================================================
                    ARTICLE 9: INDEMNIFICATION
================================================================================

9.1 Indemnification by the Receiving Party. The Receiving Party shall
    indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Disclosing Party, its
    affiliates, subsidiaries, and their respective directors, officers,
    employees, agents, successors, and assigns (collectively, the
    "Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all losses, damages,
    liabilities, costs, expenses, claims, actions, and proceedings
    (including reasonable attorneys' fees, expert witness fees, and court
    costs) arising out of or resulting from:

    (a) Any breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or its
        Representatives;

    (b) Any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information by
        the Receiving Party or its Representatives;

    (c) Any negligent or wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party
        or its Representatives in connection with the Confidential
        Information.

9.2 Unlimited Liability. The Receiving Party's liability under this
    Agreement shall be unlimited and shall not be subject to any cap or
    limitation whatsoever. The Receiving Party expressly waives any right
    to limit its liability under this Agreement.

9.3 The Receiving Party shall not be entitled to any indemnification from
    the Disclosing Party under this Agreement.

================================================================================
            ARTICLE 10: AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION
================================================================================

10.1 Audit Rights. The Disclosing Party shall have the right, upon
     reasonable notice (not less than five (5) business days), to audit,
     inspect, and examine the Receiving Party's premises, systems, records,
     files, and practices to verify compliance with this Agreement.

10.2 The Receiving Party shall provide full cooperation and access to the
     Disclosing Party's designated auditors, including access to:

     (a) Physical premises where Confidential Information is stored or
         processed;
     (b) Electronic systems, servers, databases, and cloud environments;
     (c) Personnel involved in handling Confidential Information;
     (d) Policies, procedures, and security protocols related to
         information protection;
     (e) Records of access, transmission, and storage of Confidential
         Information.

10.3 The cost of all audits shall be borne by the Receiving Party unless
     the audit reveals no non-compliance, in which case each Party shall
     bear its own costs.

10.4 The Disclosing Party may conduct audits no more than twice per calendar
     year during the Term and for a period of two (2) years following the
     expiration or termination of this Agreement.

================================================================================
                ARTICLE 11: NON-COMPETE RESTRICTION
================================================================================

11.1 Non-Compete. During the Term of this Agreement and for a period of
     twelve (12) months following the expiration or termination of this
     Agreement (the "Restricted Period"), the Receiving Party shall not,
     directly or indirectly:

     (a) Engage in, participate in, or provide any services to any
         Competitor of the Disclosing Party in the Asia-Pacific region in
         connection with projects, products, or services that are similar
         to or competitive with those contemplated under the Purpose;

     (b) Solicit, approach, or enter into any business relationship with
         any customer, supplier, or business partner of the Disclosing
         Party whose identity or business relationship was disclosed to
         the Receiving Party as Confidential Information;

     (c) Solicit, recruit, or hire any employee, consultant, or contractor
         of the Disclosing Party.

11.2 The Receiving Party acknowledges that the restrictions set forth in
     this Article are reasonable and necessary to protect the legitimate
     business interests of the Disclosing Party and that any breach of
     these restrictions would cause irreparable harm.

================================================================================
                    ARTICLE 12: PENALTY CLAUSE
================================================================================

12.1 Liquidated Damages. Without prejudice to any other rights or remedies
     available to the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party agrees to pay
     to the Disclosing Party liquidated damages in the amount of
     USD 500,000 (United States Dollars Five Hundred Thousand) per breach
     or per incident of unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information.

12.2 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the above amount represents a
     genuine pre-estimate of the loss that the Disclosing Party would
     suffer in the event of a breach and does not constitute a penalty.

12.3 The payment of liquidated damages shall not relieve the Receiving
     Party of its obligations under this Agreement or limit the Disclosing
     Party's right to seek additional damages, injunctive relief, or any
     other remedy.

================================================================================
                    ARTICLE 13: GOVERNING LAW
================================================================================

13.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
     the laws of the Republic of Singapore, without regard to its conflict
     of laws principles.

13.2 The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale
     of Goods (CISG) shall not apply to this Agreement.

================================================================================
                ARTICLE 14: DISPUTE RESOLUTION
================================================================================

14.1 Negotiation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or in
     connection with this Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt to
     resolve such dispute through good faith negotiations for a period of
     fourteen (14) days from the date of written notice.

14.2 Arbitration. If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, the
     dispute shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration
     administered by the Singapore International Arbitration Centre ("SIAC")
     in accordance with the Arbitration Rules of the Singapore International
     Arbitration Centre for the time being in force. The arbitration shall
     be conducted as follows:

     (a) Seat of Arbitration: Singapore;
     (b) Language: English;
     (c) Number of Arbitrators: Three (3). Each Party shall appoint one
         arbitrator, and the two appointed arbitrators shall appoint the
         third arbitrator who shall serve as the presiding arbitrator;
     (d) The arbitral award shall be final and binding on both Parties;
     (e) The costs of the arbitration, including the arbitrators' fees,
         shall be borne entirely by the losing Party.

14.3 The Disclosing Party reserves the right to bring proceedings in any
     court of competent jurisdiction in any country for injunctive or
     other interim relief, notwithstanding the arbitration provisions
     herein.

================================================================================
                ARTICLE 15: GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

15.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement
     between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof.

15.2 Amendment. No amendment to this Agreement shall be valid unless made
     in writing and signed by a duly authorized officer of the Disclosing
     Party.

15.3 Waiver. No waiver by the Disclosing Party of any breach shall
     constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach. The Disclosing Party's
     failure to enforce any provision shall not be deemed a waiver of its
     right to enforce such provision or any other provision.

15.4 Assignment. The Disclosing Party may freely assign or transfer this
     Agreement and its rights and obligations hereunder to any third party,
     affiliate, successor, or assign without the consent of the Receiving
     Party. The Receiving Party may not assign or transfer this Agreement
     or any of its rights or obligations hereunder without the prior
     written consent of the Disclosing Party. Any attempted assignment by
     the Receiving Party without such consent shall be null and void.

15.5 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid
     or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full
     force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be replaced by a
     valid provision that most closely achieves the original intent.

15.9 Further Assurances. The Receiving Party shall execute and deliver all

15.10 Survival. The provisions of Articles 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.2, 5.3, 6, 7, 8,
