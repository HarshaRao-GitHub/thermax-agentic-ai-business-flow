THERMAX LIMITED — HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY
Document No: HR-POL-004 | Rev: 06 | Effective: 01-April-2026
Title: Leave Policy — All Regular Employees
Applicable: All permanent employees across Thermax locations in India
Approved by: Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)

1. OBJECTIVE
This policy defines the types of leave available to Thermax employees, eligibility criteria, approval processes, carry-forward rules, and special provisions. It aims to ensure work-life balance while maintaining operational continuity.

2. SCOPE
Applicable to all permanent employees of Thermax Limited and its domestic subsidiaries. Contractual employees, consultants, and interns are governed by their respective agreements.

3. TYPES OF LEAVE

3.1 PRIVILEGE LEAVE (PL) / EARNED LEAVE (EL)
- Entitlement: 21 days per calendar year (credited monthly — 1.75 days/month)
- Eligibility: After completion of 240 days of service in a year
- Minimum duration: 1 day (half-day permitted at manager's discretion)
- Maximum continuous: 15 working days (beyond 15 days requires VP/BU Head approval)
- Carry Forward: Maximum 45 days can be carried to next year; excess lapses on 31-Dec
- Encashment: Up to 10 days per year can be encashed (at basic + DA rate); balance encashable at separation
- Advance Notice: Minimum 7 days for planned PL of 3+ days; 1 day for shorter PL
- Approval: Reporting Manager (up to 5 days); Department Head (6-15 days); VP/BU Head (>15 days)
- Sandwich Rule: If PL is taken on Friday and Monday, the intervening Saturday-Sunday are counted as PL

3.2 CASUAL LEAVE (CL)
- Entitlement: 7 days per calendar year (available from January 1)
- Eligibility: Immediately upon joining (pro-rated in first year)
- Minimum duration: Half day
- Maximum continuous: 3 days (beyond 3 days, must be combined with PL or SL)
- Carry Forward: Not permitted — unused CL lapses on 31-Dec
- Encashment: Not encashable
- Advance Notice: 1 day preferred; same-day permitted for genuine emergencies
- Approval: Reporting Manager
- Note: CL cannot be combined with PL without prior approval

3.3 SICK LEAVE (SL) / MEDICAL LEAVE (ML)
- Entitlement: 10 days per calendar year
- Eligibility: Immediately upon joining (pro-rated in first year)
- Minimum duration: Half day
- Maximum continuous: 10 days on SL; beyond requires PL or Leave Without Pay (LWP)
- Medical Certificate: Required for SL exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Carry Forward: Maximum 30 days accumulated; unused beyond 30 lapses on 31-Dec
- Encashment: Not encashable (accumulated SL used for extended illness)
- Advance Notice: Not required (by nature); employee must inform manager within 2 hours of shift start
- Approval: Reporting Manager; HR must be notified for SL > 3 days

3.4 MATERNITY LEAVE
- Entitlement: 26 weeks (for first two children); 12 weeks (for third child onward)
- Eligibility: Female employees who have worked for minimum 80 days in the 12 months preceding expected delivery
- Extension: Up to 4 additional weeks for documented medical complications
- Pay: Full pay during maternity leave
- Benefits: Health insurance, creche facility reimbursement (up to INR 5,000/month for 1 year)
- Return guarantee: Same position or equivalent upon return
- Note: Commissioning mother and adopting mother (child < 3 months) entitled to 12 weeks

3.5 PATERNITY LEAVE
- Entitlement: 5 working days
- Eligibility: Within 30 days of child's birth/adoption
- Pay: Full pay
- Approval: Reporting Manager (application to be submitted minimum 7 days before expected date)

3.6 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
- Entitlement: 5 working days for immediate family (spouse, children, parents, siblings); 3 days for extended family
- Pay: Full pay
- Documentation: Death certificate to be submitted within 15 days of return
- Approval: Reporting Manager (immediate approval expected)

3.7 COMPENSATORY OFF (COMP OFF)
- Earned when employee works on a public holiday or weekly off at management's request
- Must be availed within 30 days of earning
- Approval: Reporting Manager (must pre-approve the holiday/weekly off work)
- Maximum accumulation: 3 comp-offs at any time

3.8 LEAVE WITHOUT PAY (LWP)
- Granted only when all other leave balances are exhausted
- Maximum: 30 days per year (beyond requires MD approval)
- Impact: Proportionate deduction from salary; impacts annual bonus eligibility if LWP > 15 days
- Approval: Department Head + HR Head

4. GENERAL RULES
4.1 Leave applications must be submitted through the HRMS portal (SAP SuccessFactors)
4.2 Backdated leave applications (post-facto) permitted only for CL and SL; must be regularized within 3 working days
4.3 Unauthorized absence (no leave application and no communication for 3+ consecutive days) may result in disciplinary action
4.4 Public holidays falling within a leave period are not counted as leave days (except Sandwich Rule for PL)
4.5 Employees on probation are entitled to CL and SL only; PL is credited upon confirmation

5. LEAVE DURING NOTICE PERIOD
5.1 PL may be encashed but not availed during notice period (except with VP approval for genuine emergencies)
5.2 SL permitted with medical certificate
5.3 CL not permitted during notice period

6. CONTACT DURING EXTENDED LEAVE
Employees on leave exceeding 10 days must provide a contactable phone number and respond to critical communications within 24 hours.

7. REVISION HISTORY
Rev 06: Updated maternity leave to 26 weeks per Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act; added paternity leave
Rev 05: Increased PL from 18 to 21 days; introduced comp-off policy
Rev 04: Added sandwich rule clarification; updated approval matrix
