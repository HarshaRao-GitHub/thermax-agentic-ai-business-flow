SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION
M/s Saurashtra Cement Ltd. (SCL) invites proposals from qualified vendors for the design, engineering, manufacturing, supply, erection, testing, and commissioning of an Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for our cement manufacturing facility located at Ranavav Cement Works, Porbandar District, Gujarat.

The ESP will be installed to control particulate emissions from the Kiln/Raw Mill circuit in compliance with applicable environmental regulations including CPCB emission norms (notified under Environment Protection Act, 1986) and Gujarat Pollution Control Board (GPCB) consent conditions.

This is a turnkey supply contract. The vendor shall be responsible for end-to-end execution including design, manufacturing, logistics, erection, refractory/insulation (if any), testing, and commissioning of the ESP system, and shall guarantee performance as specified herein.

SECTION 2: SCOPE OF WORK
The scope includes (but is not limited to):
2.1 Process design, sizing, and engineering of the complete ESP system
2.2 Mechanical design, fabrication, surface treatment, and supply of all structural and internals components
2.3 Collecting electrode plates (CE) and discharge electrode frames (DE) — complete sets with alignment provisions
2.4 Rapping systems — electromagnetic rappers for collecting electrodes; tumbling hammer/pneumatic rappers for discharge electrodes
2.5 Hopper system — pyramidal hoppers with heating arrangement, level detectors, and evacuation system (chain conveyor or rotary airlock valves)
2.6 Casing and roof — gas-tight, insulated, with proper expansion joints and access doors for each field
2.7 Gas distribution system — perforated plates and vane-type flow distribution devices at inlet and outlet
2.8 Electrical systems including Transformer-Rectifier (TR) sets with automatic voltage control
2.9 Microprocessor-based ESP controller with spark rate optimization and back-corona detection
2.10 Rapper control system — programmable rapper sequencing with individual rapper diagnostics
2.11 Integration with existing plant DCS/PLC system
2.12 Power distribution panel, MCC, cable trays, junction boxes, and all cabling (power + control)
2.13 Ductwork from existing duct termination point to ESP inlet and from ESP outlet to existing ID fan/chimney
2.14 Support structure and foundations — design of ESP support structure (fabrication of structure by vendor; civil foundations by SCL based on vendor's loading data)
2.15 Erection and commissioning at site including all temporary works, scaffolding, and construction equipment
2.16 Performance testing and guarantee demonstration as per IS 5765 / relevant standards
2.17 Training of plant O&M personnel — minimum 2 batches, 3 days each
2.18 Complete documentation package — engineering drawings (GA, assembly, fabrication), installation manual, O&M manual, spare parts manual, test certificates, material certificates

3.2 ESP Design Requirements
Type: Dry, horizontal flow, weighted wire (rigid frame) or rigid discharge electrode ESP
Number of Fields: Minimum 4 fields (vendor may propose 5 fields if required for guaranteed performance)
Specific Collection Area (SCA): Vendor to specify; minimum expected 90-120 m2/(m3/s) for cement application
Collection Efficiency: >= 99.93% (based on inlet 45 g/Nm3 and outlet 30 mg/Nm3)
Effective Plate Height: 12-15 m (vendor to optimize)
Gas Velocity: 0.8-1.2 m/s between plates (vendor to optimize based on SCA and field count)
Plate Spacing: 300-400 mm (vendor standard)
Aspect Ratio (length/height): >= 1.2

3.2.1 Collecting Electrodes
Material: Cold-rolled carbon steel, minimum 1.2 mm thick
Profile: Roll-formed stiffened profile (C-profile, U-profile, or equivalent)
Surface Treatment: Shot-blasted, zinc metallized spray (minimum 100 microns) on external surfaces exposed to gas

3.2.2 Discharge Electrodes
Type: Rigid frame with spiral or star-type emitting elements
Material: Stainless steel SS 304 emitting elements on mild steel frame (EXOTIC MOC: SS 304 emitting elements for corrosion resistance in SO2 environment)
Tensioning: Spring-loaded self-aligning system

3.2.3 Rapping System
Collecting Electrode Rapping: Electromagnetic impulse rappers — individually controllable
Discharge Electrode Rapping: Tumbling hammer or pneumatic type
Rapper Control: Microprocessor-based, fully programmable intensity and frequency per field; integrated diagnostics for rapper health monitoring
Anti-re-entrainment: Proper baffle arrangement between fields; optimized rapping sequence

3.2.4 Gas Distribution
Inlet gas distribution: Multi-stage perforated plates + turning vanes
Distribution quality: RMS deviation <= 15% (as per ICAC EP-7 guidelines)
Model study: Vendor shall conduct CFD or physical model study and provide gas distribution report

3.2.5 Hopper Design
Shape: Pyramidal with minimum 60-degree valley angle
Heating: Electric or steam trace heating to prevent moisture condensation and bridging
Level Detectors: Capacitance or vibrating fork type — 2 levels per hopper (high and high-high)
Evacuation: Chain conveyor or rotary airlock valves (vendor to propose)
Poke holes and inspection doors on each hopper

3.2.6 Casing, Insulation & Structural
Casing: Gas-tight welded construction, minimum 6 mm thick
Insulation: 100 mm mineral wool with aluminum cladding (0.5 mm thick)
Expansion Joints: Fabric expansion joints at inlet and outlet duct connections
Access: Man-doors and inspection doors for every field, accessible walkways and platforms at electrode level, hopper level, and roof level
Structural: Self-supporting steel structure designed for seismic zone III per IS 1893

3.3 Electrical & Control System
3.3.1 Transformer-Rectifier (TR) Sets
Type: Single-phase, oil-immersed, silicon-controlled rectifier type
Rating: One TR set per field (minimum 4 TR sets)
Input: 415V AC, 3-phase, 50 Hz (fed from MCC provided by vendor)
Output DC Voltage: 50-80 kV (vendor to size based on plate spacing and application)
Output DC Current: Vendor to specify per field; minimum 800 mA per field
Features: Automatic voltage control (AVC) with spark rate optimization, back-corona detection, intermittent energization mode, power-saving mode during low-load operation

3.3.2 ESP Controller
Type: Microprocessor-based, dedicated ESP controller
Features Required:
- Automatic spark rate control (target: 20-60 sparks per minute per field)
- Back-corona detection and automatic response
- Rapping optimization based on opacity feedback
- Trend monitoring and data logging (minimum 30 days on-board)
- Remote access via Ethernet/Modbus TCP for integration with plant DCS
- Multi-language HMI with 10-inch color touchscreen
- Event and alarm logging with timestamp
- Opacity (CEMS) feedback integration for closed-loop control

3.3.3 Integration with Plant DCS
Communication Protocol: Modbus TCP/IP or Profibus DP (as per existing plant standard)
Signals to DCS: ESP operating status, field-wise voltage and current, opacity reading, TR set status, rapper status, hopper level alarms, all trip and fault alarms
Control from DCS: ESP start/stop, energization mode selection, rapper enable/disable
Minimum I/O Points: 200 hardwired + 300 soft signals via communication

3.3.4 Remote Monitoring Capability
Web-based or cloud-based dashboard for real-time ESP performance monitoring
Historical data access and trend analysis
Alarm notification via email/SMS
Vendor to provide remote diagnostics support for minimum 2 years post-commissioning

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE GUARANTEES

Vendor must guarantee the following performance parameters during the performance guarantee test conducted as per IS 5765 or equivalent standard:

4.1 Outlet Emission: <= 30 mg/Nm3 at reference conditions (10% O2, dry basis, NTP) — GUARANTEED
4.2 Target Emission: <= 25 mg/Nm3 — preferred; vendor to state if achievable
4.3 Pressure Drop across ESP: <= 20 mmWC (at design gas flow, clean to 6-month operating condition)
4.4 Power Consumption: <= 0.3 kWh per 1000 Am3 of gas treated (including TR sets and auxiliary power)
4.5 Availability: >= 98% over any rolling 12-month period (excluding planned shutdowns)
4.6 Gas Distribution: RMS deviation <= 15% at model study stage and <= 25% at site measurement
4.7 Collection Efficiency: >= 99.93%

4.8 Penalty Clauses
Outlet emission exceeding 30 mg/Nm3 but <= 40 mg/Nm3: LD of 0.5% of contract value per mg/Nm3 per month until rectified
Outlet emission exceeding 40 mg/Nm3: Vendor to rectify at own cost within 90 days or face contract termination with full refund
Power consumption exceeding guarantee by > 10%: LD calculated on additional energy cost over 10-year NPV basis
Availability below 98%: LD of 0.5% of contract value per percentage point below guarantee, capped at 5% of contract value

SECTION 5: ENVIRONMENTAL & REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

The system must fully comply with:
5.1 Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) emission norms for cement industry — 30 mg/Nm3 for PM (as per Environment Protection Amendment Rules, 2022)
5.2 Gujarat Pollution Control Board (GPCB) consent conditions and any additional state-specific requirements
5.3 Environment Protection Act, 1986 and rules thereunder
5.4 Continuous Emission Monitoring System (CEMS) requirements — vendor to provide CEMS integration points (opacity meter probe flange on ESP outlet duct)
5.5 National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS) — vendor to ensure stack emission contributes within ambient limits using dispersion modeling if requested
5.6 Applicable international standards: IEC for electrical, ICAC for ESP design/performance testing
